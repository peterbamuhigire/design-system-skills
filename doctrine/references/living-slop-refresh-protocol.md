# Reference: Living AI-Slop Refresh Protocol

AI slop is not a fixed list. The visible tells move as models, templates, social platforms,
vendor defaults, and design fashion move. This protocol keeps the design engine from freezing
around yesterday's artifacts.

Use it when updating `ai-slop-taxonomy.md`, `ai-slop-banned-fonts.md`, slop audit skills, or
any doctrine that names "current" AI tells.

---

## Authority split

- **Design engine owns:** visual, typographic, layout, brand, interface, product-feature, and
  handoff consequences.
- **Digital-research engine owns:** source discovery, source evaluation, claim discipline,
  evidence grading, and written/cultural slop classification.

The design engine may update doctrine only after a digital-research pass has separated:

- observed current tells,
- evidence strength,
- plausible but unverified claims,
- design-engine consequences.

## Refresh triggers

Run a refresh when any of these are true:

- A user says definitions of AI slop have changed, or asks whether a current artifact is slop.
- A model/vendor/template default becomes visibly common in generated output.
- A banned font or visual tell is disputed.
- A public backlash, marketplace flood, platform policy, or design-community discussion changes
  what clients recognise as low-effort AI output.
- A slop reference is older than one quarter and is being used for a high-stakes public artifact.

## Digital-research handoff

Route to the digital-research engine before changing doctrine:

1. Load `skills/source-evaluation/SKILL.md`.
2. Load `skills/source-evaluation/references/evidence-discipline.md`.
3. Use `skills/anti-ai-slop/SKILL.md` and `skills/ai-slop-audit/SKILL.md` for textual/cultural
   slop framing.
4. Require sources to be separated by type: primary platform/vendor source, design-practitioner
   commentary, public incident, marketplace observation, social evidence, or weak anecdote.
5. Record uncertainty explicitly. Do not convert weak signals into hard bans.

## Design update rubric

For every proposed doctrine change, decide which bucket it belongs in:

| Bucket | Evidence needed | Design-engine action |
|---|---|---|
| Hard ban / critical tell | Repeated current convergence, public backlash, vendor/template default, or strong multi-source evidence | Add to banned list or critical audit checklist with date and reason label |
| Watchlist / caution | Emerging pattern with plausible evidence but limited breadth | Add as watchlist note; require deliberate justification before use |
| Contextual risk | Harmful only in specific sectors, channels, or artifact types | Add context-specific gate, not universal ban |
| Rejected claim | Weak, circular, outdated, or purely aesthetic dislike | Do not add; record if needed to prevent re-litigation |

## Required update shape

Every update must state:

- `Observed shift:` what changed in the world.
- `Evidence grade:` strong, moderate, weak, or rejected.
- `Design consequence:` what the engine will now reject, watch, or require.
- `Scope:` typography, image, video, UI/product, brand, document, or writing handoff.
- `Date checked:` ISO date.

## Non-negotiables

- Do not treat AI-vendor recommendations as approval authority.
- Do not promote a font, image style, layout, or UI trope because AI tooling recommends it.
- Do not duplicate digital-research writing guidance inside this engine; cross-cite it.
- Do not leave "current" 2026/2027 claims without a refresh trigger or source trail.
