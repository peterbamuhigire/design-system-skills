# Premium UI/UX Gate

Use this gate before shipping user-facing design work. Score each category 0-10. Anything below 8 blocks premium claims.

## 1. Business Clarity

- The user understands the product purpose, value, and next action quickly.
- The interface supports the actual business model: lead generation, SaaS activation, ecommerce conversion, retention, support reduction, or operational efficiency.
- Critical trust signals are visible where decisions happen.

## 2. Visual Quality

- Layout, typography, color, imagery, and spacing feel deliberate.
- The design has a clear visual voice appropriate to the buyer and domain.
- It avoids generic AI styling, visual clutter, and cheap decorative habits.

## 3. Usability And Efficiency

- Frequent tasks are fast, obvious, and forgiving.
- Navigation matches the user's mental model.
- Forms, tables, search, filters, and actions reduce work instead of adding friction.

## 4. Content And Communication

- Copy is specific, outcome-oriented, and concise.
- Labels and actions describe what will happen.
- Empty, error, loading, and success states help users continue.

## 5. Accessibility And Inclusiveness

- Keyboard, screen reader, contrast, focus, motion, touch target, and text scaling requirements pass.
- Color is not the only carrier of meaning.
- The design works for the least powerful supported device and slow network profile.

## 6. Data And Decision Quality

- Charts, tables, KPIs, and dashboards answer real questions.
- Comparisons, baselines, targets, and exceptions are explicit.
- Visual encoding is accurate enough for the decision.

## 7. Platform And Production Fit

- Web, Android, and iOS patterns respect their platform conventions.
- Components are tokenized, documented, reusable, and state-complete.
- Implementation is responsive, performant, and screenshot-verified.

## Blocking Defects

- No useful mobile layout.
- Inaccessible primary workflow.
- Unclear primary action on a revenue-critical page.
- Dashboard that hides or distorts the core business decision.
- Native app screen that ignores platform navigation or touch conventions.
- Design system with no component states.
- Visual direction that contradicts the brand, audience, or product economics.
